Detailed Subject-Wise Syllabus

The RUHS Medical Officer examination syllabus is primarily based on the standard MBBS curriculum as recognized by the National Medical Commission (NMC). Below are the core subject areas heavily tested in the examination:

1. Basic Medical Sciences

  • Anatomy: Gross anatomy, Microanatomy, Embryology, and Neuroanatomy.
  • Physiology: General Physiology, Nerve-Muscle, Blood, Cardiovascular System, Respiratory System, Gastrointestinal System, and Endocrinology.
  • Biochemistry: Cell and Sub-cellular structures, Enzymes, Carbohydrate, Lipid, and Protein metabolism.

2. Para-Clinical Sciences

  • Pathology & Microbiology: General Pathology, Systemic Pathology, Bacteriology, Virology, Mycology, and Parasitology.
  • Pharmacology: General Pharmacology, Systemic Pharmacology, and Antimicrobial Drugs.
  • Forensic Medicine: Medical Jurisprudence, Toxicology, and Forensic Psychiatry.

3. Clinical Sciences

  • General Medicine: Infections, Cardiovascular, Respiratory, Gastrointestinal, Neurology, and Hematology.
  • General Surgery: Principles of Surgery, Trauma, Abdominal Surgery, and Orthopedics.
  • Obstetrics & Gynaecology (OBG): Antenatal care, Normal and Abnormal Labor, Gynecological disorders, and Family Planning.
  • Pediatrics: Neonatology, Growth and Development, Nutrition, and Common childhood illnesses.
  • Preventive & Social Medicine (PSM): Epidemiology, Public Health Administration, Biostatistics, and National Health Programs.
  • Other Key Specialties: Ophthalmology, Otorhinolaryngology (ENT), Dermatology, Psychiatry, and Anesthesiology.
